Oaxaca, Mexico

Tours and Attractions

Mezcal production

See how mezcal is produced and taste as many samples as you can take. - Recommended full day -

Recommended full day tour to dive deep into the art and history of mezcal. Depending on the activities happening during the day of your visit, you will participate in the production process while you understand the history, social and economical context of mezcal and how this drink became such an essential part of local and national culture. Hear about the radical cultural shifts that have driven mezcal from an obscure local product to a national phenomenon and at the end of your visit, try as much mezcal as you can taste to find out which maguey is right for you.

Cooking workshop

Learn how to make the local foods.- Recommended full weekend -

This course is recommended as a full weekend activity. Upon your arrival you will be taken to a local market to buy all of the ingredients that you will need [price not included] and begin your immersion into the local smells and flavours of Oaxaca. You will then begin the production of 3 to 5 sets of dishes, like “mole negro”, coloradito and handmade tortillas besides learning how to make small treats like Oaxacan hot chocolate and pumpkin dessert. You will participate in the entire “mole” production process, from the dehidration of the chile to the preparation of corn up to the mixture of the more than 15 ingredients that make up this unique dish.

Alebrije workshop

Paint your own pre-carved alebrije sculpture. - Recommended half day -

Recommended half day course in which you will receive a pre-carved alebrije sculpture [price not included] and they show you the different techniques used to paint the fine detail of this handicraft, particularly the use of syringes that allow for higher precision. You have an afternoon to paint the sculpture according to your own taste while receiving support and guidance from the local artisan family. Understand the origin and history of the community, the construction of it’s church and stone water pools and learn more about their organizational structure and unique political views of this independent Oaxacan indigenous people.

Organic agricultre

Learn how to make organic produce and be self-sustainable. - Recommended half day -

This is recommended as a half day course. Learn how to use worms and other natural fertilizers to make healthy, natural, organic produce. Understand how different plants help to protect each other from plagues and predators and how one can harvest all he needs to survive without ever using chemicals or fertilizers or any form of external supplements.
