Communities

YAHUICHE

Oaxaca, Mexico

Tucked away in the high woods of the Sierra Juárez, Yahuiche is more than mind blowing mountain views and day-long treks following century old paths through the valleys. With a population of less than 200 members, this autonomous indigenous community is a lesson in what collaboration, democracy and sustainability really means, besides being home to the best handmade bread in the region. 10 minutes off the highway, you will also find "La Loma Organica" ("The Organic Hill"), and independent project operated by locals that will show how living can be 100% sustainable without compromising. La Loma Organica includes a restaurant, pool and cabins that are available for rent.

Tours and Attractions

Water

4-5 hour hike to the highest lookout point in the region and visits to community projects.

View the entire sierra from one of it’s highest peaks and marvel at breathtaking sunsets. See how the community has transformed the abundance of the region’s water into one of it’s most valuable resources. Visit local family projects such as an organic farm and flower greenhouses.

La voladora

5-7 hour trekking around the valley of the sierra and along the river bed.

One of the most intense and rewarding treks in the region. Hike down to the sierra’s valley, cross hundred year old stone bridges, swim in the river, visit old monasteries and caves in the sides of the mountain and stand on the Voladora stone, a beautiful lookout in the center of the valley with amazing views of the entire sierra.

Origins

Half day tour focused in local history and main sights in the community center

Understand the origin and history of the community, the construction of it’s church and stone water pools and learn more about their organizational structure and unique political views of this independent Oaxacan indigenous people.
